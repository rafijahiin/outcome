UNFPA Bangladesh CP10 Indicators Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of sexual and reproductive health, gender equality, and population dynamics across 10 priority districts

About This Dashboard

This dashboard presents a thematic analysis of UNFPA Bangladesh's Country Programme 10 (CP10) indicators, tracking progress across sexual and reproductive health, gender equality, and population dynamics. The visualizations highlight key trends, regional disparities, and progress toward 2026 targets across 10 priority districts in Bangladesh.

Maternal Health Trends

Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) Trends and Targets

Data Sources:

ICPD_to update - brb (MMR data 1986-2023), 2. CP10_Outcome (Baseline 165/100,000, Target 100/100,000 by 2026)

Bangladesh has made significant progress in reducing maternal mortality, with MMR declining from 648 in 1986 to 136 in 2023. However, the country is slightly off-track to achieve the 2026 target of 100/100,000 live births. The 2023 value of 136 shows improvement from the 2022 value of 153, indicating positive momentum.

Family Planning Indicators

Contraceptive Prevalence Rate (mCPR) Trends

Data Sources:

ICPD Trend Analysis Bangladesh (mCPR data 1993-2022), 2. CP10_Outcome (Baseline 63.9%, Target 80% by 2026)

Modern Contraceptive Prevalence Rate has shown steady improvement over the past three decades, rising from 36.2% in 1993 to 65.6% in 2021. However, there was a slight decline to 62.1% in 2023. Significant efforts are needed to reach the 2026 target of 80% coverage with modern methods.

Postpartum Family Planning (PPFP) by District

Data Sources:

Sum_Facility_District (PPFP data Jan-Jun 2023), CP10_Output (PPFP indicators)

Cox's Bazar leads in PPFP services with 3,972 cases in Jan-Mar 2023, followed by Jamalpur (1,856) and Gaibandha (1,034). Regional disparities are evident, with some districts showing significantly lower uptake of postpartum family planning services.

Gender-Based Violence Services

GBV Medical Care Services Across Districts

Data Sources:

Sum_Facility_District (GBV medical care data Jan-Jun 2023), CP10_Output (GBV service indicators)

Cox's Bazar reported the highest number of GBV medical care cases (157 in Jan-Mar, 186 in Apr-Jun 2023), likely reflecting both higher incidence and better service availability. Several districts reported zero cases, which may indicate underreporting or service gaps rather than absence of GBV.

Adolescent Health

Adolescent Birth Rate Trends

Data Sources:

ICPD_to update - brb (Adolescent birth rate 1995-2023), 2. CP10_Outcome (Baseline 74/1000, Target 60/1000 by 2026)

Adolescent birth rate has declined significantly from 127 in 1995 to 73 in 2023. While progress has been made, the current rate of 73 remains above the 2026 target of 60, indicating need for continued focus on adolescent sexual and reproductive health services.

Child Marriage Prevalence by District

Data Sources:

2. CP10_Outcome (District-level data from MICS 2019), National baseline 51.4%

Child marriage prevalence varies significantly across districts, with Bagerhat (69.8%), Gaibandha (62%), and Sirajganj (61.5%) showing the highest rates. Cox's Bazar (36.6%) and Bandarban (31.7%) have comparatively lower rates. The national target is to reduce this to 30% by 2026.

Service Coverage Comparison

Skilled Birth Attendance by District

Data Sources:

2. CP10_Outcome (District-level skilled birth attendance from MICS 2019), National baseline 59%, Target 72% by 2026

Significant disparities exist in skilled birth attendance across districts, with Dhaka (79.9%) and Bagerhat (65.2%) showing higher coverage, while Bandarban (18.7%) and Jamalpur (28.2%) lag considerably. These disparities highlight the need for targeted interventions in low-performing districts.

Post-Abortion Care Services

Post-Abortion Care (PAC) Services by District

Data Sources:

Sum_Facility_District (PAC data Jan-Jun 2023)

Cox's Bazar provides the highest volume of PAC services (488 in Jan-Mar, 519 in Apr-Jun 2023), followed by Patuakhali and Sirajganj. Several districts show minimal PAC service provision, indicating potential gaps in comprehensive abortion care services.

Menstrual Regulation Services

Menstrual Regulation (MR) Services by District

Data Sources:

Sum_Facility_District (MR data Jan-Jun 2023)

Cox's Bazar leads in MR service provision with 92 cases in Jan-Mar and 86 in Apr-Jun 2023. Noakhali shows a significant increase in the second quarter (from 68 to 164 cases). Several districts report minimal MR services, potentially indicating access barriers or reporting issues.

Fertility Trends

Total Fertility Rate (TFR) Historical Trends

Data Sources:

ICPD_to update - brb (TFR data 1974-2023)

Bangladesh has experienced a remarkable fertility transition, with TFR declining from 6.8 in 1974 to 2.17 in 2023. This represents significant progress in family planning acceptance and reproductive health services, though the rate has slightly increased from the low of 2.04 in 2019-2020.

Antenatal Care Coverage

Four or More Antenatal Care Visits by District

Data Sources:

CP10_Output (ANC4 coverage data 2022-2023)

ANC4 coverage varies significantly across districts, with Jamalpur (31.4%) and Cox's Bazar (19.5%) showing higher rates in 2023, while Barguna (2.3%) and Patuakhali (4.0%) have critically low coverage. These disparities highlight the need for targeted interventions to improve antenatal care utilization.

Progress Towards 2026 Targets

CP10 Outcome Indicators: Baseline vs Progress vs Target

Data Sources:

2. CP10_Outcome (Baseline, progress, and target data for key indicators)

Progress toward 2026 targets varies across indicators. While maternal mortality reduction is on track, contraceptive prevalence and skilled birth attendance need acceleration. Adolescent birth rate reduction and child marriage elimination require significant efforts to achieve targets. GBV service reporting shows improvement but likely underrepresents actual need.
